## Specific Risk Factors

### Concentration Risk
Following a single trader concentrates risk. Consider:
- Following multiple traders
- Diversifying across market types
- Limiting exposure to any single position

### Correlation Risk
Multiple traders may hold similar positions, creating hidden concentration. Their seemingly diversified strategies may actually be highly correlated.

### Timing Risk
Entry and exit timing matters. Even following a successful trader:
- Your entry price may differ
- Market conditions may have changed
- Liquidity may affect execution

### Behavioral Risk
Following traders may create psychological challenges:
- Overconfidence in high-scoring traders
- Panic during drawdowns
- Abandoning strategy at wrong times
